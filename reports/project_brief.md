# Bayesian Project Brief

## Goal

Finish a clear undergraduate Bayesian data analysis project by April 20, 2026 using a public dataset and a modest modeling scope.

## Recommended project directions

### Option 1. Palmer Penguins Hierarchical Model

- Research question: how much do species, island, and sex explain variation in penguin body mass or bill length?
- Bayesian angle: compare pooled, no-pooling, and hierarchical models.
- Why it works: small clean dataset, easy plots, low implementation risk.

### Option 2. Bike Sharing Count Model

- Research question: how do weather and calendar variables affect bike rental demand?
- Bayesian angle: compare Poisson and negative binomial models with posterior predictive checks.
- Why it works: intuitive predictors and a strong count-data story.

### Option 3. Heart Disease Logistic Model

- Research question: which patient features are most associated with heart disease risk?
- Bayesian angle: Bayesian logistic regression with coefficient uncertainty and prior sensitivity.
- Why it works: socially meaningful problem with a manageable feature set.

## Suggested choice

If the team wants the safest path, start with Palmer Penguins.

If the team wants a slightly more applied story, choose Bike Sharing.
